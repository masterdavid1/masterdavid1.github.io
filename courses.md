## <span id="ece-295-hardware-design">ECE 295 Hardware Design</span>

Project 1, RF transceiver Design filter receiver end, rectifier, mixer, etc. Both the design and the physical product met the target parameters and won awards. PCB drawing was carried out using Altium Designer, and simulation was conducted using two software, TI Multisim and Pspice.

## <span id="ece-241-digital-systems">ECE 241 Digital Systems</span>

Algebraic and truth table representations of logical functions and variables. Optimize combinatorial logic and multi-level logic optimization. Transistor-level design of logic gates The propagation delay and timing of gates and circuits. Learn the Verilog hardware language and apply it to the Intel Quartus Prime tool to program the FPGA.

## <span id="ece-212-circuit-analysis">ECE 212 Circuit Analysis</span>

Circuit design course, involving resistance Circuit, storage Circuit, operational amplifier, Circuit Analysis, introduced the concept of ac power system, active power and reactive power, power factor, the complex power and three-phase analysis. For sinusoidal steady-state analysis, the topics include phasor analysis, impedance and admittance. The course is simulated using TI Multisim.

## <span id="ece-244-programming-fundamentals">ECE 244 Programming Fundamentals</span>

Programming c + + Programming design. Provide the foundation for programming using object-oriented programming languages. The topics include classes and objects, inheritance, encapsulation and polymorphism.

## <span id="ece-330-quantum--semiconductor-physics">ECE 330 Quantum & Semiconductor Physics</span>

Semiconductor Physics, Quantum mechanics course learned two parts. The first part covers the fundamentals of quantum mechanics, including the 1D and 3D Schrodinger equations, the photoelectric effect, wave-particle duality, de Broglie's conjecture, and the fundamentals of quantum statistics. The second part restates the fundamentals of device physics, including band theory, pn junction hybridization principle, and tunneling effect, etc.

## <span id="ece-335-introduction-to-electronic-devices">ECE 335 Introduction to Electronic Devices</span>

Device based solid state physics, p-n junction, semiconductor diodes, semiconductor transistor, MOS capacitor, etc. The simulation was carried out using Synopsys Sentaurus TCAD to model device characteristics. Personal lab reports in this course can be found in the Labs tab.

## <span id="ece-437-vlsi-technology">ECE 437 VLSI Technology</span>

Introduction of integrated circuit manufacturing Technology and process, integrated circuit design and advanced semiconductor devices. The course experiment is generally divided into two parts. The first part uses Synopsys Sentaurus TCAD software for the modeling and simulation of semiconductor devices, doped devices and analyzes the cross-sections. The second part is the layout design, where Cadence Vitruoso is used to draw the circuit diagram and layout for DRC and LVS. Personal lab reports in this course can be found in the Labs tab.

## <span id="ece-331-analog-electronics">ECE 331 Analog Electronics</span>

The building blocks include single stage transistor gain cell, following by current mirror, differential and multi-stage amplifiers, IC bias technology and output stage design. The frequency response of the amplifier at low, medium and high frequencies. Feedback amplifier analysis. The stability and compensation technology of negative feedback amplifiers. The experiment combines the actual construction of basic modules with LTSPICE simulation. Personal typed and organized course notes can be found below.

## <span id="ece-334-digital-electronics">ECE 334 Digital Electronics</span>

IC Digital design technology of Digital circuit. The focus is on the design of transistor-level logic gates, CMOS: device modeling, IC processing, Spice simulation, simplification of layout rules, storage functions SRAM, DRAM, EEPROM. The course simulation uses LTSPICE for circuit simulation and simple layout drawing.

## <span id="ece-472-engineering-economics">ECE 472 Engineering Economics</span>

Engineering Economics required course. The economic evaluation and argumentation of engineering projects and investment plans were discussed. The concept of cost; Financial and cost accounting; Depreciation; The time value and compound interest of money; Inflation Capital budget Equity, bond and loan financing; Income tax and after-tax cash flow in the project proposal for engineering projects;

## <span id="ece-430-analog-integrated-circuits">ECE 430 Analog Integrated Circuits</span>

Review of MOSFET semiconductor device equations. Noise in electronic devices. Review of single-stage amplifiers and frequency response, including noise analysis. Basic CMOS op amp. Op amp compensation. Advanced op amp circuits: telescopic and folded-cascode op amps. Fully-differential op amps. Common mode feedback.

## <span id="ece-314-power-electronics-and-motors">ECE 314 Power Electronics and motors</span>

High-efficiency energy conversion via switched-mode power electronic circuits: design and steady-state modeling of DC/DC converters, DC/AC converters using pulse-width modulation. AC power quality and power factor, including non-sinusoidal currents. Energy conversion via magnetic devices: Faraday's law for time varying fields, transformer and inductor modelling and design. Introduction to electromechanical energy conversion: Lorentz Force, concepts of energy, co-energy, forces between ferromagnetic materials carrying flux, simple magnetic actuators, introduction to synchronous machines.

## <span id="ece-442-introduction-to-micro--and-nano-fabrication-technologies">ECE 442 Introduction to Micro- and Nano-Fabrication Technologies</span>

The physical principles of optical lithography, electron-beam lithography, alternative nanolithography techniques, and thin film deposition and metrology methods. The physical and chemical processes of wet and dry etching. Cleanroom concepts and safety protocols. Sequential micro-fabrication processes involved in the manufacture of microelectronic and photonic devices. Imaging and characterization of micro- and nano-structures. Inspection using Scanning Electron Microscopy and Transmission Electron Microscopy.
